The Mission


Seebook is our proposal to establish a bridge between the traditional physical editions and the digital format that:

Provides a tool to the traditional publishing industry to adapt to the digital era.

Invigorates the cultural market generating a third dissemination channel.

Facilitates authors and musicians the promotion of their work.

Brings the digital world to a generation more used to the tangible.



The Team


Rosa Sala Rose

Non-fiction writer and translator. BA in German Studies and PhD in Romance Philology by the University of Barcelona. After engaging in literary translation and editing German classics (especially Goethe and Thomas Mann), she now writes non-fiction and is an independent scholar. Her speciality is Nazism from the perspective of the history of mentalities. In English she has published so far Lili Marlene. The Biography of a song. She also gives lectures, collaborates with several media and maintains a personal blog.

Rosa Sala Rose is the creator of Seebook as the result of her knowledge of the publishing world, since as a writer and translator she had to deal with all the agents involved and knows from first hand the challenges that the emergence of ebooks means to the sector.



Jordi Pérez Solà

He has a Degree in Sociology and a Master of Science in Management by the University of Barcelona. He has a graduate degree in communication. He has been a public manager in several companies and foundations: vice-president of the private health foundation of Sant Llàtzer, vice-president of the environmental management company Terrassa Neta, S.A, executive vice-president of the Eco-Equip company, S.A.M and president of the municipal waste management GRUVOSA.



Simon Effing

CTO, software architect and full stack developer. Simon is specialised in lean and agile software projects, in particular web and mobile applications. He has extensive experience operating in an international business environment under challenging startup conditions. Previously he has worked for several startups and projects in Germany, Singapore, United States, Canada, UK, Spain and China in various sectors including financial, health care and telecommunication services.
